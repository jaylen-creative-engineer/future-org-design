# Competitive landscape

Software tiers, representative vendors, consulting structure, and the collective capability gap versus an end-to-end ODaaS loop.

## Competitive tiers

Three rough bands: enterprise deep modeling, mid-market blended platforms, and lightweight charting.

Rough segmentation:

- **Enterprise-grade** — Orgvue, Nakisa, Ingentis: transformation programs, often consultant-led.
- **Mid-market** — ChartHop, Functionly, Agentnoon/Dayforce: faster value, modular pricing.
- **Lightweight** — Organimi, Pingboard: visualization-first, shallow analytics.

## Who owns which slice

Vendors usually own **one slice**: analytics OR design OR planning; ONA is elsewhere. ODaaS claims the **full loop** — [[closed-loop-value-chain#Closed-loop pipeline]].

## Consulting market structure

MBB, Big 4, Accenture own methodology and rates; boutiques (OTM, AlignOrg, Kates) go deep at small scale.

**Tier 1:** MBB, Big 4, Accenture — Star Model, TOM, high rates.

**Tier 2–3:** Boutiques — depth without platform leverage.

Consulting delivers **prescriptive, high-touch** work — [[strategic-gaps#White space map (2×2)]] — not reusable software.
