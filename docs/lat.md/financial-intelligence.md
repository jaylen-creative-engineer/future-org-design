# Financial intelligence

Financial intelligence for ODaaS: connect org design choices to economic impact so leaders can compare scenarios using cost, capacity, risk, and growth leverage.

## Executive snapshot

Reorganizations are capital-allocation decisions with structural consequences. Teams need a way to evaluate design options using consistent economics, not disconnected spreadsheets and one-off consulting artifacts — [[buyers-and-pain#Consultant reliance]], [[market-and-tam#Workforce planning disconnect]].

ODaaS financial intelligence should make scenario decisions auditable and prescriptive, linking organizational changes to measurable value — [[odaas-core#ODaaS product thesis]], [[strategic-gaps#Prescriptive analytics gap]].

## Intelligence capability map

1. **Baseline economics** — current labor and structure costs across teams and layers.
2. **Scenario deltas** — compare proposed structures against baseline assumptions.
3. **Implementation burden** — estimate transition effort, sequencing risk, and disruption.
4. **Sensitivity controls** — model ranges instead of false precision.
5. **Growth leverage** — connect design quality to execution speed and operating capacity.

Financial intelligence must incorporate formal hierarchy and collaboration realities where possible — [[ai-and-data-layer#Organizational network analysis (ONA)]], [[strategic-gaps#ONA and informal organization]].

## Decision dashboards

Minimum decision surfaces:

- **Baseline vs scenario** — FTE, span/layer, and cost movement by option.
- **Risk and readiness** — implementation burden and dependency-sensitive risk.
- **Sensitivity view** — effect of assumption ranges on projected outcomes.
- **Portfolio comparison** — side-by-side scenario ranking for committee decisions.

The objective is decision support for the full pipeline, not standalone reporting — [[closed-loop-value-chain#Closed-loop pipeline]], [[competition#Who owns which slice]].

## Data inputs

Financial decision quality depends on reliable structural and workforce source data.

- Organizational structure and role-level metadata.
- Compensation bands and workforce mix assumptions.
- Hiring/attrition and vacancy dynamics.
- Scenario lifecycle events and recommendation artifacts.
- Financial governance rules for approvals and thresholds.

## MVP slices

These slices prioritize auditable economics before deeper FP&A integration.

- **FIN-01 Scenario delta summary** — structured economics export for each scenario.
- **FIN-02 Span/layer cost lens** — show cost and coordination implications.
- **FIN-03 Sensitivity bands** — bounded assumptions with explicit confidence levels.
- **FIN-04 Readiness signal** — combine economics with implementation risk for sequencing.

## Risks and constraints

- Precision risk: financial outputs must communicate ranges and provenance.
- Access risk: sensitive data requires role-aware visibility and controls.
- Misalignment risk: cheapest structure is not always highest-value structure.

Financial intelligence should remain coupled to closed-loop outcomes and continuous redesign, not episodic budget cycles — [[buyers-and-pain#Continuous design gap]], [[closed-loop-value-chain#Why it matters]].
