## 1. Executive Summary

### The Opportunity

Organizational design is broken. Ninety-two percent of business leaders prioritize it as critical, yet only 11% feel confident they can execute it successfully — an 81-point confidence deficit that represents a structural gap in the market ([Deloitte HC Trends Report, 2021](https://www.deloitte.com/nl/en/services/consulting/perspectives/designing-the-future-the-transformational-role-of-organization-design-centers-of-excellence.html)). Today, a Fortune 500 company pays McKinsey $1,193/hour to redesign its structure in a six-month engagement that has less than a 25% chance of fully achieving its objectives — and then repeats the exercise three years later when the structure decays again. ODaaS replaces this episodic, expensive, consultant-dependent model with an intelligence-first platform that enables continuous, data-driven organizational design at a fraction of the cost and a multiple of the speed.

### Why Now — Timing Factors

1. **The AI transformation forcing function.** Only 21% of organizations using generative AI have redesigned their workflows — the single factor most correlated with EBIT impact ([McKinsey State of AI 2025](https://www.libertify.com/interactive-library/state-of-ai-2025-mckinsey-report/)). Every enterprise deploying AI needs to redesign its structure to capture the value. AI is redesigning job roles in 3–6 month cycles; most workforce planning operates on 12–24 month cycles. The gap between those two timelines is the market.

2. **Consulting is failing at scale.** Fewer than 25% of org redesigns fully achieve their stated objectives. The average McKinsey project costs $1.5–2.5M and creates recurring dependency rather than organizational self-sufficiency. A wave of sophistication among HR buyers is driving demand for tooled, continuous alternatives.

3. **Market fragmentation is an entry signal.** The top 10 org design consulting firms hold less than 5% of market share — even McKinsey, the largest single player, holds ~1% ([Research and Markets, 2026](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/reports/5972771/organization-change-management-consulting-market)). No dominant platform exists at the intersection of AI, org design, people analytics, and workforce planning.

4. **AI in HR is normalizing prescriptive recommendations.** Workday Illuminate, SAP Joule, and Visier's Vee have trained enterprise buyers to expect AI-driven recommendations in HR workflows. The natural extension — AI recommendations for organizational structure itself — is the white space no incumbent owns.

5. **Team Topologies and SBOs are expanding the market.** The Team Topologies framework (170,000+ books sold; 2nd edition 2025) is spreading beyond tech into marketing, healthcare, and finance. Skills-based organizations require continuous structural adaptation — a use case static tools cannot serve.

### The Core Gap in the Market

No platform currently integrates the full organizational design workflow end-to-end with AI at the core:

```
People Analytics → Org Design → Workforce Planning → Implementation → Monitoring → (repeat)
```

Current tools own only one slice. Visier does analytics but not design. Orgvue does design but not analytics at depth. Anaplan does planning but not structure. ONA tools (Viva Insights, Worklytics) exist in a completely separate category. And nothing prescribes what you should do next. ODaaS closes this loop — making it the first platform to take an organization from diagnostic insight through structural recommendation through implementation to continuous monitoring, with intelligence built at every layer.

## 3. Competitive Landscape

### 3.1 Enterprise-Grade Platform Overview

The org design software market sits at the intersection of HR technology, financial planning, and strategic consulting. It fragments into three tiers:

- **Enterprise-grade systems** (Orgvue, Nakisa, Ingentis) — deep analytical/modeling platforms targeting Fortune 500 transformation programs
- **Mid-market platforms** (ChartHop, Functionly, Agentnoon) — blending org charts with workforce planning analytics
- **Lightweight charting tools** (Organimi, Pingboard, Lucidchart) — primarily visualization with limited analytical depth

### 3.5 What None of Them Do — The Collective Gap

Even taken together, today's tools leave major gaps that an ODaaS platform could fill:

1. **No platform integrates formal structural modeling with informal network data (ONA).** ONA tools (Viva Insights, Worklytics, Polinode) are completely separate from design tools.
2. **No platform uses AI to proactively recommend org structures** — current AI is reactive (answers questions, generates dashboards) not prescriptive.
3. **No platform models the work layer** (actual activities, task allocation) alongside the structural layer (boxes and lines).
4. **No platform models the operating model** — decision rights, governance, ways of working, process architecture — not just the org chart.
5. **No platform supports continuous org design** with a closed-loop learning system; all tools support episodic transformation.
6. **No platform models human + AI team composition** as a first-class design dimension.
7. **No platform connects org design decisions to change management execution** (the "last mile").
8. **No platform natively integrates external labor market intelligence** (skills supply, salary benchmarks, competitor org data) into structural modeling.
9. **No platform supports full matrix and cross-functional org design** at depth.
10. **No platform is accessible to line managers** for distributed, governed micro-design — most tools serve only central OD specialists or consultants.

## 5. AI & Intelligence Layer

### 5.1 Current AI Applications in Org Design

AI's application to organizational design has evolved from static headcount planning to continuous, data-driven workforce strategy. As of 2025–2026, machine learning models are routinely used for:

- **Predictive attrition modeling:** Flagging 20%+ probability of turnover in cohorts six months in advance
- **Dynamic headcount modeling:** Rolling forecasts against capital and operating plans (replacing annual snapshots)
- **Skills-gap forecasting:** Comparing current capabilities against 3–5 year business scenarios at task level
- **Agentic workflow automation:** AI agents initiating job postings or learning events after human approval
- **Structural flattening simulation:** AI assessing which of 85 knowledge worker roles could be automated and generating alternative organizational structures ([Sciety/preprint, May 2025](https://sciety.org/articles/activity/10.21203/rs.3.rs-6649663/v1))

**McKinsey's central challenge (State of AI 2025):** Only 21% of organizations using generative AI have redesigned workflows, yet workflow redesign is the single factor most correlated with meaningful EBIT impact. "AI is 20% algorithms and 80% organizational rewiring." ([McKinsey State of AI 2025, via Libertify](https://www.libertify.com/interactive-library/state-of-ai-2025-mckinsey-report/))

**The "triaxial organization" model** (CIO Journal, March 2026): As AI matures, a new structural model is emerging — a hierarchical-functional axis for stability, a human network axis for innovation, and a cognitive AI axis where AI systems operate as a structural layer to stabilize processes and orchestrate decisions. ([CIO, March 2026](https://www.cio.com/article/4150146/from-hierarchies-to-triaxial-organizations-designing-ai-driven-structures.html))

### 5.4 Academic Frameworks

#### Team Topologies (Skelton & Pais)

Conway's Law (1967) forms the theoretical foundation: organizations design systems that mirror their communication structures. Team Topologies advocates the **"Inverse Conway Maneuver"** — deliberately restructuring teams to match desired software architecture.

**Empirical validation:** A 2019 Microsoft Research study of 25 large software projects found a statistically significant correlation (r=0.74, p<0.001) between organizational communication patterns and software dependency structures. Projects where organizational communication closely matched system architecture showed **60% fewer defects and 40% faster feature delivery**. ([LinkedIn/Andre, April 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/conways-law-digital-transformation-organizational-andre-oxqwe))

**AI connection in 2nd edition:** The 2025 second edition explicitly addresses AI's role in team design, emphasizing cognitive load as a design principle and the shift from viewing organizations as "efficient machines" to "flourishing ecosystems."

#### Galbraith's Star Model

Five mutually reinforcing design levers with complete interdependence:

| Design Lever | Description | AI Application (2025) |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Strategy** | Where to play and how to win; defines critical capabilities | AI provides real-time market signals, competitive intelligence, scenario analysis |
| **Structure** | Formal roles, reporting lines, governance | ONA reveals informal structures; AI models optimal spans |
| **Processes** | Flow of work and information; lateral coordination mechanisms | AI automates lateral coordination, information routing, exception detection |
| **Rewards** | Metrics, incentives, recognition (OKRs, compensation) | Skills-based compensation AI dynamically assesses proficiency and market rates |
| **People** | Roles, skills, talent flows, workforce planning | AI-powered skills inference, talent matching, development recommendations |

**Core Galbraith insight:** Structure is necessary but insufficient. The star only works when all five points are aligned. ([Umbrex/Galbraith Star Model, Jan 2026](https://umbrex.com/resources/frameworks/organization-frameworks/galbraith-star-model/))

#### Optimal Team Size Research

| Framework | Optimal Size | Basis |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Dunbar's innermost circle** | ~5 | Maximum stable "intimate" relationships with deep mutual commitment |
| **Amazon Two-Pizza Rule** | 5–8 | Team fed by two pizzas; minimizing communication complexity |
| **Scrum** | 3–9 | Agile team design; minimum viable cross-functional capability |
| **Dunbar's "sympathy group"** | ~15 | Inner circle for mutual support; upper boundary for manager-to-report ratio in complex work |
| **Dunbar's "band"** | ~50 | Stable work group without formal sub-structures |
| **Dunbar's number** | ~150 | Maximum social connections humans can maintain; structural sub-division becomes mandatory |
| **Gore's manufacturing threshold** | ~150 | Gore Industries found cohesion/teamwork declined when factories exceeded 150 people |

**AI's impact on spans of control:** AI tools materially reduce coordination load, changing the span-of-control equation. Four mechanisms: (1) AI monitors work patterns and surfaces exceptions, (2) automated task redistribution balances workloads, (3) AI-generated analytics give managers real-time team visibility, (4) standardized workflows reduce interpersonal coordination needs. ([LinkedIn/Shastri, Jan 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/rethinking-span-control-age-ai-dheeraj-shastri-8xoqe))

#### Human-AI Teaming Research

**PNAS Nexus, February 2026** — Major interdisciplinary study proposing a framework for human-AI team design:
- Human-AI teams with humans in the minority experience reduced trust and weaker shared knowledge
- Role clarity and specialization mitigate coordination risks
- AI excels at scalable execution and reliable monitoring; humans excel at adaptive role negotiation and creative restructuring
- "Humans design team structures, adapt roles, and manage exceptions; AI ensures reliable execution, monitoring, and auditability" ([PNAS Nexus, Feb 2026](https://academic.oup.com/pnasnexus/article/5/3/pgag030/8490283))

### 6.2 Top Pain Points

#### Pain Point 1: Not Knowing When or How to Restructure

Leaders struggle to diagnose whether structural problems are the root cause of performance challenges — or whether the issue lies in strategy, people, or process. Key triggers that force a restructuring decision:

- **M&A:** Failure rates consistently 70–90%, often because of underestimated organizational complexity
- **Executive transitions:** 27–46% of executive transitions fail within two years, frequently because structure doesn't adapt
- **Strategy shifts:** 70% of change projects fail due to poor execution when strategy and structure fall out of sync
- **Technology disruption:** McKinsey projects ~15% of the global workforce (~400 million workers) could be displaced by automation by 2030
- **Organic performance decay:** Teams spending more time coordinating across boundaries than doing core work

Yet leaders often delay: "Most corporate turnarounds fail before they start — not because of market conditions or flawed tactics, but because senior leadership fails to initiate the turnaround in the first place." ([Fortune, Nov 2025](https://fortune.com/2025/11/19/why-most-corporate-turnarounds-fail-before-they-begin-restructuring-reorgs/))

#### Pain Point 2: Lack of Data for Org Decisions

Most org design decisions are made based on intuition, political pressures, or consultant recommendations rather than rigorous data:

- Only 12% of companies involve employees in org design (Orgvue data)
- Only 11% use advanced techniques like organizational network analysis and activity modeling
- Only 9% of companies actually use agile organizational models despite widespread claims ([Orgvue Journey to Agile](https://www.orgvue.com/content/uploads/sites/2/2022/07/OD-22_03-DefGuide-to-Organization-Design-Journey-to-Agile-Definitive-Guide-1.pdf))

#### Pain Point 3: Restructuring Is Expensive, Slow, and Disruptive

- A global manufacturing firm case spent "nearly a year refining the new structure for a 300-person team, drafting countless versions of the org chart. By the time it was finalized, company strategy and leadership had shifted — the structure never came to life." ([Growthspace](https://www.growthspace.com/blog/organizational-restructuring-best-practices))
- **91% of executives** report having too many conflicting priorities
- **50% productivity drop** documented in one case during the restructuring period: "like cleaving one hand from every worker in an instant"
- Only 50% of leaders can confidently assess the success of recent organizational changes (CEB, 2016)

#### Pain Point 4: Misalignment Between Structure and Strategy

- Only **14% of executives** believe the traditional hierarchical org model makes their organization highly effective ([Deloitte Global Human Capital Trends, 2017](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/talent/human-capital-trends/2017/organization-of-the-future.html))
- **Strategic misalignment wastes more than half a company's resources** (Harvard Business Review)
- McKinsey: mismanaged strategy-structure gaps can cost companies up to **10% of annual revenue** — $1 billion annually for a $10B enterprise

#### Pain Point 5: Over-Reliance on Expensive Consultants

- McKinsey senior partners: **$1,193/hour** (2024 GSA rate); BCG: **$1,116/hour**
- Typical engagement: **$500K–$1.25M** for 12 weeks; $1.5M–$2.5M at full MBB rates
- What is learned "is often not adequately shared with the organization — which means the organization doesn't actually learn valuable insights it can use down the road. Instead, it remains dependent on consultants." ([NPR/"The Big Con," 2023](https://www.npr.org/sections/money/2023/03/21/1162242773/need-a-consultant-this-book-argues-hiring-one-might-actually-damage-your-institu))
- Increasingly, companies are "accepting that change is constant and are adopting the approach of having full-time org design teams on the payroll" ([Nakisa, 2025](https://nakisa.com/blog/organizational-design-trends-in-large-enterprises/))

#### Pain Point 6: Loss of Institutional Knowledge During Reorgs

- **Voluntary turnover spikes 15–25%** in the six months following a restructure ([Work Institute Retention Report](https://cloverera.com/turnover-after-restructure/))
- Organizations can lose **18+ months of combined institutional knowledge** from a single restructuring event
- Effect is invisible on most dashboards: "Productivity may hold. Engagement scores may remain stable. Headcount may be fully backfilled. Yet the organization becomes increasingly dependent on a shrinking circle of individuals." ([LinkedIn/Richard Ray Jr.](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/how-organizations-quietly-lose-institutional-richard-ray-jr--b9vze))

### 7.3 Operating Model Design — The Biggest Whitespace

**The problem:** Organizational design is not just about who reports to whom. Effective org design includes:

- **Decision rights** — who decides what, at what level
- **Governance models** — how committees, councils, and boards function
- **Ways of working** — agile, functional, matrix, product-led, etc.
- **Process architecture** — how core processes flow across teams
- **Culture and behavioral norms** embedded in the design

No current platform meaningfully addresses this **operating model layer** that sits between strategy and structure.

Evidence of the gap:
- Deloitte's own Eight Dimensions framework explicitly includes decision rights, processes, and governance — but no software makes these tangible and measurable
- Team Topologies addresses this conceptually for tech orgs but offers no software
- McKinsey's "nine golden rules" of redesign include governance and decision rights as critical elements, but there's no tool that makes them executable
- Consulting firms charge the highest fees precisely for operating model work because it's the most complex and consequential — and the most underserved by tooling

### 7.4 Human + AI Org Design for the AI Era

Organizations are deploying AI agents into their workflows and need to answer structural questions that no current tool addresses:

- Which roles should be redesigned for human-AI collaboration?
- Where should we reduce headcount vs. redeploy?
- How should governance change for AI-augmented decision-making?
- What is the right span of control when managers are supported by AI monitoring?
- How should team types be redefined when AI absorbs execution and humans focus on judgment?

**BCG identified three archetypes of AI-driven restructuring (September 2025):**
1. **Streamliners:** Collapsing roles, phasing out coordination layers, building lean pods
2. **Collaborators:** Redesigning for hybrid human-AI teaming
3. **Transformers:** Rebuilding entire job families around AI-human teaming; flattening the pyramid; creating new job ladders that reflect AI orchestration
([BCG, Sep 2025](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/ai-is-outpacing-your-workforce-strategy-are-you-ready))

The **McKinsey State of Organizations 2026** (10,000+ executives across 15 countries and 16 industries) identified AI/technology infusion as the first of three tectonic forces reshaping organizations. ([McKinsey, Feb 2026](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/our-insights/the-state-of-organizations))

**ODaaS advantage:** A platform purpose-built for the AI era treats human-AI teaming as a first-class design dimension — not an afterthought bolted onto existing org chart tools.

### 7.5 Continuous vs. Episodic Design

**The central structural tension in the market:**

Traditional consulting and software alike are designed for episodic, project-based work. But the pace and frequency of organizational change now requires continuous capability.

**Evidence of the episodic model failing:**
- 44% of redesign efforts run out of steam after getting underway (McKinsey)
- Organizations that try to keep up with change through high-frequency episodic redesigns "cannot effectively adapt, often accelerating a downward spiral with each episodic change attempt" ([Synthegrate](https://synthegrate.com/index.php/knowledge-areas/change-management/163-linking-episodic-and-continuous-change-perspectives-to-understand-dynamic-change-processes))
- By the time a crisis triggers a formal redesign, the organization has already been changing informally — often in misaligned directions

**The continuous org design requirement:**
- **Operational org design tools** for managers and HRBPs to make small, tactical adjustments without waiting for large-scale transformation initiatives
- **Span of control monitoring:** AI-driven insights identifying when spans are drifting in real time
- **Real-time feedback loops:** Continuous organizational health data (engagement, decision velocity, collaboration patterns) vs. annual surveys
- **Scenario simulation on demand:** "What-if" environments showing cascading effects before committing

Nakisa (2025): "Increasingly, companies are accepting that change is constant and are adopting the approach of having full-time org design teams on the payroll as opposed to hiring external consultants to lead org transformations at irregular intervals." ([Nakisa, 2025](https://nakisa.com/blog/organizational-design-trends-in-large-enterprises/))

### 7.6 The Informal Organization — ONA Not Integrated Into Design Tools

**Research finding:** Informal networks and decision rights are "twice as powerful as structure in driving organizational effectiveness" (McKinsey). Yet:

- Every major platform (Orgvue, Nakisa, ChartHop, etc.) models only the **formal** organizational structure
- ONA tools (Viva Insights, Worklytics, Polinode, Humanyze) are completely **separate** from org design platforms
- Only Panalyt includes ONA as one analytics module, but it is not integrated with design tools
- The #1 cited org design failure mode: overreliance on org charts that "only depict formal hierarchies"

**The opportunity:** A platform that combines formal org modeling with informal network data — so that org design decisions are informed by actual collaboration flows, not just who reports to whom. This represents the most direct gap in the entire competitive landscape.
